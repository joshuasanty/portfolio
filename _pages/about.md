## Welcome

I'm Joshua Santy, a recent graduate of Missouri University of Science and Technology with a Master of Science in Applied Mathematics emphasizing Statistics.

My interests lie in data analysis, statistics, machine learning, and scientific computing. I enjoy using data to solve practical problems, communicate insights, and support decision-making.

During my time at Missouri S&T, I worked as an Undergraduate Research Assistant in computational condensed matter physics, where I developed Python scripts, performed statistical analysis, and utilized high-performance computing resources to study the electronic properties of amorphous tin oxide materials. I also worked as a Library Assistant and was honored as Outstanding Student Worker of the Year, which strengthened my communication, organization, and teamwork skills. In addition, I have completed statistics-focused projects involving data analysis, visualization, and modeling, which helped me build a strong foundation in applying quantitative methods to real-world problems.
